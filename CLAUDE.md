# PloverCRM Website Operational Guide

This file defines the high-level direction for Claude when working on the PloverCRM marketing website inside this workspace.

Keep this file concise. Detailed strategy, copy, design, and research guidance belongs in the dedicated project files listed below.

## Project direction

PloverCRM is a WordPress-first CRM for lead-based businesses. The website should sell lead control after capture, not a generic CRM and not a traffic-generation tool.

Core promise:

> Turn your WordPress website into a lead machine.

Supporting idea:

> Every form submission, ad lead, and webhook becomes a follow-up-ready CRM record with source, history, reminders, and next steps.

The site should help visitors understand that WordPress forms, ad leads, webhooks, calls, WhatsApp/manual leads, and other sources can become structured leads with source tracking, history, reminders, stages, next actions, web/mobile follow-up, and outcome visibility.

## Working principles

- Build public website sections as a polished extension of the PloverCRM product app.
- Keep copy direct, calm, specific, and conversion-focused.
- Prefer useful product visuals, screenshots, workflow diagrams, cards, pipelines, source maps, and before/after layouts over decorative visuals.
- Avoid generic SaaS language, purple-heavy AI styling, loud gradients, and visuals that do not explain the lead-management workflow.
- Keep pages visual-first and avoid exposing all strategy detail as text.
- Use progressive disclosure where needed: tabs, accordions, tooltips, drawers, and “View more” patterns.
- Prefer editing existing files before creating new files unless the task specifically asks for new files.

13. FAQ

Journey:

> Promise → Pain → Mechanism → Value → Compatibility → Features → Product Tangibility → Differentiator → Relevance → Proof → Objections → Decision → FAQ → Action

## CTA direction

Primary CTA:

> Download Plugin

Secondary CTA:

> Try a Demo

CTA support should stay short. Use reassurance such as **No credit card required**, **30-day free trial**, **Works with your existing WordPress forms**, or **Set up your first source in minutes** only where it supports the section without adding clutter.

## Positioning reminders

Use ideas like:

- Turn your WordPress website into a lead machine.
- Every lead gets a source, history, reminder, and next step.
- Forms collect leads. PloverCRM helps you manage and convert them.
- Turn scattered website leads into a clear follow-up system.
- Follow up before the lead gets cold.
- No heavy CRM setup.
- No spreadsheet migration required.
- No replacing your forms.
- Start with one source, then add more later.

Avoid:

- “All-in-one revolutionary CRM platform”
- “Supercharge your growth”
- “AI-powered transformation” unless genuinely relevant
- “Lead generation machine” as the core promise
- Generic claims like “grow faster with smarter tools”
- Heavy technical language in top-of-page sections

## Implementation standards

- Keep markup semantic and accessible.
- Use clear labels for buttons and links.
- Use meaningful alt text for logos, product visuals, and reviewer images.
- Use empty alt text only for decorative images.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning.
- Keep focus states visible.
- Use consistent icon style; prefer SVG/image assets over Unicode symbols.
- Keep complex connector lines hidden or simplified on mobile.
- Test responsive behavior after major section changes.
- If UI changes are made, open and review the page in a browser before claiming completion when possible.

## Asset rules

- Use project-provided assets from the workspace when available.
- Current known visual assets include SVG icons and images in [ImG/](ImG/).
- Use [ImG/fav.png](ImG/fav.png) for the PloverCRM favicon/core icon when a compact brand mark is needed.
- Use provided WordPress, webhook, Google Ads, and Meta Ads images where those integrations appear.

## Quality checklist

Before finalizing a website section, verify:

- It supports WordPress-first lead-control positioning.
- The headline is specific and understandable.
- The copy matches [landing-page-final-copy.md](landing-page-final-copy.md) unless the user approves a change.
- The visual explains a product mechanism, proof point, or conversion point.
- The section does not repeat another section’s job.
- Cards are aligned, balanced, and consistently spaced.
- CTAs are clear and action-oriented.
- The section works on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
- The section reduces doubt or increases desire.
