## Strategic job

Move visitors from “we already collect leads” to “we need a follow-up system.”

## Core message

Forms collect leads. Spreadsheets store lists. PloverCRM helps your team manage what happens after capture with source, owner, stage, reminder, history, next action, mobile access, and outcome visibility.

## Key comparison angles

- Capture vs follow-up
- Entry storage vs complete CRM record
- Notification vs ownership
- Submitted fields vs source/history/context
- Spreadsheet rows vs stages/reminders/next actions
- Manual updates vs visible team workflow
- Desktop-only handling vs web/mobile follow-up
- No outcome feedback vs lead outcome visibility

## Recommended outline

1. **Hero**
   - Frame the problem: getting the lead is not the same as managing it.

2. **Quick answer**
   - Use a short direct answer that explains when forms/spreadsheets are enough and when CRM becomes necessary.

3. **Quick comparison table**
   - Columns: Form entries, Spreadsheets, PloverCRM.
   - Rows: capture, storage, source tracking, owner, reminders, history, stage, mobile follow-up, outcome tracking.

4. **What forms do well**
   - Forms are excellent for collecting inquiries, quote requests, demo requests, and contact details.

5. **Where form entries break down**
   - Form entries often lack ownership, reminders, source history, next action, team visibility, and outcome tracking.

6. **What spreadsheets do well**
   - Spreadsheets are flexible for lists, manual tracking, and simple reporting.

7. **Where spreadsheets break down**
   - They require manual updates, are easy to forget, do not create reminders, and make ownership/history messy.

8. **What a CRM adds after capture**
   - Source, owner, stage, reminder, notes, activity, next action, and mobile access.

9. **How PloverCRM works with existing WordPress forms**
   - No need to replace existing forms.
   - Start with one source and add more later.

10. **Before/after workflow table**
   - Before: scattered entries, inboxes, spreadsheets, missed follow-up.
   - After: CRM records, reminders, owners, stages, source tracking, outcomes.

11. **Signs you have outgrown forms and spreadsheets**
   - Leads are missed.
   - Follow-up depends on memory.
   - Nobody knows who owns the next step.
   - Source/campaign details get lost.
   - Mobile follow-up is inconsistent.

   - Do I need to migrate spreadsheets?
   - Is a CRM too much for a small team?
   - How does PloverCRM help after form submission?
