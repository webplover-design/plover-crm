## Strategic job

Help WordPress businesses decide whether they need a WordPress-first CRM layer or a broader hosted SaaS CRM platform.

This should be the flagship comparison article. It can mention many options in one useful place without creating thin one-off competitor pages.

## Core message

A SaaS CRM can be powerful, but many WordPress businesses first need a focused way to manage captured website leads, source context, reminders, history, mobile access, and outcomes without turning a simple lead workflow into a heavy external CRM rollout.

## Competitors/categories to discuss

- **WordPress CRM/plugins:** Jetpack CRM, FluentCRM, Groundhogg, WP ERP
- **Broad SaaS CRM:** HubSpot, Zoho CRM, Pipedrive, Salesforce
- **Agency/all-in-one platform:** GoHighLevel
- **Mobile-first lead follow-up:** Privyr
- **Current alternatives:** form entries, inboxes, spreadsheets

## Key comparison angles

- Where leads start: WordPress forms, ads, webhooks, calls, manual entries
- Setup effort and time to first useful lead record
- WordPress form workflow and plugin ecosystem fit
- Source/campaign tracking
- Owner, stage, reminder, next action
- Lead history and activity timeline
- Native mobile follow-up vs desktop/admin-only workflow
- Pricing model: plugin/subscription vs per-seat SaaS vs implementation/admin overhead
- Data ownership/control
- Reporting and outcome visibility
- When a full SaaS CRM is genuinely better
